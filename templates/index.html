The Spectrum Times

A comparative model that determines shift in the political spectrum of a country in the past decade.


For this project, we arranged all the data from the research Lucas did. This involved thoroughly checking through different reputable sources, including Reuters and BBC to remove biases and report accurate descriptions of countries' recent controversial policies. Using this data, we assigned a rank to the countries we surveyed, and used the results to establish a ranking. Upon compiling the research, we processed the data online using Numpy and Pandas.


We used a range of metrics to measure how much a country's political spectrum has shifted in the past 10-15 years.

Immigration Reforms

Rhetoric on immigration policies tend to align with the populist bent of a nation's leadership.

LGBTQ+ Rights

In the United States, for example, LGBTQ+ rights are a proxy for the social views espoused by a country's government in the last decade. Attitudes toward LGBTQ+ rights are indicators of social activism in a nation, attitudes towards change, and the degree to which traditional religion exerts social control on community life.

Foreign Relations

Looking at a country's stance on foreign relations informs us of the populist leanings of a country. 21st century populism frequently comes at odds with the notions of globalization and free trade.

Economic Policies

A nation's economic policies can indicate the priorities of its current government. Economic right-leaning policies drive deregulation, corporate freedom, reduced taxes, and protectionism.

Religious Tolerance

For example, in the United States, President Donald Trump's administration had limited religious tolerance Countries shifting to the right may prioritize the word of religious institutions and a return to traditional cultural elements.
